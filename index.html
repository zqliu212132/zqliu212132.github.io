About Me

I am currently a visting assistant professor of Urban Studies at Worcester State University.

I got my PhD in Community and Regional Planning at the University of Texas at Austin in 2024, advised by Dr. Ming Zhang. I obtained M.S. in Statistics at the University of Texas at Austin in 2021, and Master's Degree in Urban and Regional Planning at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2018.

My current research focuses on the economic impacts of transportation investments, spatial inequality measurements, and urban transit networks. My research projects respond to growing inequality in the megaregions and metropolitans and the role of transportation planning to mend the gap. I specialize in multiple economic impact analysis models, such as the multi-regional input-output (MRIO) model, the computable general equilibirum (CGE) model, and the Hedonic price model.

In addition, I have explored the methods in urban simulation with open sourcing data and the agent-based model. I am looking forward to solving urban problems with civic techonology. With the urban design background, I am interested in place-based policies for urban and rural revitalization.
